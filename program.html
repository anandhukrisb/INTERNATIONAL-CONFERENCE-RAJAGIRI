Conference Program

A comprehensive three-day experience featuring eleven concurrent streams, exploring the intersection of social work with health and mental health care.

Program Overview

Spanning three days, the Conference main program will be offered over eleven concurrent streams each day. The diverse range of topics covered in each stream will explore the intersection between social workers and Health and Mental Health care.

Blending keynote and featured speakers, plenary and concurrent sessions and workshops, the Conference program will bring forward new concepts, discuss existing practices and give attendees the most up-to-date subject knowledge available.

What to Expect

Keynote & Featured Speakers

Hear from internationally renowned experts who are shaping the future of social work in health and mental health. Our keynote presentations will challenge conventional thinking and inspire innovative approaches to practice.

Plenary Sessions

Engage in comprehensive discussions that bring together diverse perspectives on critical issues. These sessions foster collaborative thinking and knowledge exchange among delegates from around the world.

Concurrent Sessions

Choose from eleven parallel streams covering specialized topics. Each stream offers deep dives into specific practice domains, emerging research, and innovative methodologies in health and mental health social work.

Interactive Workshops

Participate in hands-on workshops designed to build practical skills. Learn new techniques, tools, and methodologies directly applicable to your professional practice from experienced facilitators.

Session Formats

The conference offers varied session formats to accommodate different learning preferences and professional development needs:

  • Keynote Presentations (60 minutes) – Inspiring talks from leading international experts addressing major themes and challenges in the field
  • Plenary Sessions (90 minutes) – Large-group discussions featuring panels of experts exploring critical issues from multiple perspectives
  • Concurrent Sessions (45 minutes) – Focused presentations on specific research findings, innovative practices, and case studies across 11 parallel streams
  • Workshops (2 hours) – Interactive, hands-on sessions for skill development and practical application of new methodologies
  • Panel Discussions (75 minutes) – Multi-perspective explorations of complex issues featuring diverse expert viewpoints
  • Networking Sessions – Dedicated time for connecting with colleagues, building relationships, and informal knowledge exchange
