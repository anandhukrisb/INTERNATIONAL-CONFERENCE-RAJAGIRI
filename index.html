Navigating a Changing World: Empowering Minds and Transforming Lives

The 10th International Conference on Social Work in Health and Mental Health (ICSWHMH) was held from Tuesday 19, November to Friday, 22 November in vibrant Melbourne. This hybrid event gathered over 1100+ delegates from Australia and around the world, offering an unparalleled opportunity to connect, learn and innovate in the field of social work.

Welcome from the AASW CEO

#2027 ICSWHMH

Dear Colleagues,
I am delighted to welcome each of you as we embark on the 10th International Conference on Social Work in Health and Mental Health (10th ICSWHMH). This event takes advantage of being a hybrid experience, wherever you are and in the vibrant city of Melbourne.

Hosting over 600 national and international delegates, this Conference provides a unique platform to exchange insights on the current challenges and trends in the social work sector. I look forward to forging new connections, learning from leading experts, and exploring the latest ideas available.

CEO
AASW

Keynote Speakers Announced

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Professor of Social Work at University of Melbourne. Expert in mental health policy and community care systems.

Read More

Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Dr. Jenkins has over 20 years of experience in social work and mental health policy. She has published numerous papers on community care systems and is a leading voice in advocating for mental health reform. Her current research focuses on the intersection of social policy and individual well-being.

Prof. David Chen

Director of Global Health Initiatives. Focusing on cross-cultural social work practices in developing nations.

Read More

Prof. David Chen

Professor Chen leads global initiatives aimed at improving social work practices in developing nations. His work emphasizes the importance of cultural competence and sustainable development. He has worked extensively in Southeast Asia and Africa.

Dr. Emily Carter

Clinical Psychologist and Author. Specializes in trauma-informed care and resilience building.

Read More

Dr. Emily Carter

Dr. Carter is a renowned clinical psychologist specializing in trauma-informed care. She is the author of several best-selling books on resilience and recovery. Her workshops provide practical tools for practitioners working with trauma survivors.

Mr. James Wilson

Social Justice Advocate. Dedicating his career to housing equality and urban social work.

Read More

Mr. James Wilson

Mr. Wilson has dedicated his career to fighting for housing equality. As a social justice advocate, he works tirelessly to address urban poverty and homelessness. He is a frequent speaker at national conferences on urban planning and social equity.
